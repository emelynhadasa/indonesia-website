Borobudur

A UNESCO World Heritage site and one of the most iconic and significant Buddhist monuments in the world.

  • Historical and Cultural Significance

    9th-century Mahayana Buddhist temple

    A 9th-century Mahayana Buddhist temple located in Central Java, Indonesia. It was built during the reign of the Sailendra Dynasty, between the 8th and 9th centuries. The temple is a representation of the Buddhist cosmology, and it consists of nine stacked platforms, six square and three circular, topped by a central dome. It is adorned with 2,672 relief panels and 504 Buddha statues.

  • Architectural Marvel

    Gupta architecture of India

    Borobudur is renowned for its impressive architecture and design. It reflects a blend of Indonesian indigenous architecture and the Gupta architecture of India. The temple's structure resembles a mandala, with a main dome at the center surrounded by concentric terraces.

  • Relief Panels

    8th-century Java
    The walls of Borobudur are adorned with intricate relief panels, telling the story of Buddhist cosmology and the life of Siddhartha Gautama, who later became the Buddha. The reliefs also depict scenes of daily life in 8th-century Java.

  • Cultural and Religious Syncretism

    Cultural and religious syncretism

    Borobudur reflects a cultural and religious syncretism between the prevailing animist traditions of Java and Buddhism. The fusion of these elements is evident in the temple's design and the inclusion of indigenous elements in its construction.

  • Restoration and UNESCO Recognition

    20th century

    Borobudur underwent major restoration efforts in the 20th century, particularly in the 1970s and 1980s, to address issues like volcanic ash damage, weathering, and decay. The restoration project was a collaborative effort involving UNESCO and the Indonesian government. In 1991, Borobudur was designated as a UNESCO World Heritage Site, recognizing its cultural and historical significance. It is also one of the most visited tourist attractions in Indonesia.
