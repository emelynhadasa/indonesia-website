Labuan Bajo

A town located on the western tip of Flores Island in Indonesia, known for being the gateway to Komodo National Park, home to the famous Komodo dragons.

  • Gateway to Komodo National Park

    Unique biodiversity

    Labuan Bajo is the primary gateway for visitors to Komodo National Park, a UNESCO World Heritage site. The park is known for its unique biodiversity, including the Komodo dragon, the world's largest lizard.

  • Scuba Diving and Snorkeling Hub

    Rich marine life

    The waters around Labuan Bajo are known for their rich marine life and vibrant coral reefs. The area has become a popular destination for scuba diving and snorkeling, attracting enthusiasts from around the world.

  • Culinary Scene

    Various and international culinary

    Labuan Bajo offers a diverse culinary scene, with a variety of seafood restaurants, local eateries, and international cuisine options. Visitors can enjoy freshly caught seafood while taking in views of the harbor.

  • Boat Tours and Island Hopping

    Nearby islands, beaches, dive sites

    The town serves as a hub for boat tours and island hopping adventures. Visitors can explore the nearby islands, beaches, and dive sites. Boat trips often include stops at Rinca Island and Padar Island, among others.

  • Sunset Views

    Picturesque sunset views

    Labuan Bajo is known for its picturesque sunset views over the harbor. Many visitors and locals gather at vantage points to enjoy the stunning sunsets, creating a relaxing and scenic atmosphere in the evenings.
